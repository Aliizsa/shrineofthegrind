Lore of the Order

Origins

In the hush between battles, the first Keepers inked the laws of work upon parchment. They wrote of hands that never stray, of paths walked twice to make them true, and of small stones stacked into towers that greet the dawn.

Our line endures not by thunder, but by the soft rhythm of tasks finished. Let those who seek spectacle look elsewhere; here, the lamp is trimmed for long nights.

Vows

  • Guard the Work from haste and vanity.
  • Measure twice. Lay once. Return tomorrow.
  • Share tools. Teach quietly. Credit fairly.

Doctrine

The Tower

Rise by courses, not leaps. Every tier is a promise kept to the last.

The Wheel

What is begun returns. Maintain, refine, revisit—until the work is quiet in your hands.

The Ledger

Record with honesty. Praise the small completion as you would a banner raised.
